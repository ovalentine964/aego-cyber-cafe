How to File KRA Returns in 2026 — Step by Step Guide

Filing your KRA tax returns doesn't have to be stressful. Here's everything you need to know for the 2026 tax season.

Who Needs to File KRA Returns?

Every Kenyan with a KRA PIN is required to file annual tax returns — even if you had no income. This is called filing nil returns. If you earned income (employed, business, rental, etc.), you must declare it.

What You Need

  • Your KRA PIN certificate
  • KRA iTax portal login credentials (itax.kra.go.ke)
  • P9 form (if employed) or business records (if self-employed)
  • Mpesa/bank statements for income verification

Steps to File KRA Returns

  1. Log in to the KRA iTax portal at itax.kra.go.ke
  2. Select "Returns" → "File Return"
  3. Choose your tax type (Income Tax — Resident Individual)
  4. Fill in your income details and deductions
  5. Submit and download your acknowledgment receipt

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Filing late — penalty is KSh 2,000 per month
  • ❌ Wrong PIN — make sure you use your personal PIN, not your employer's
  • ❌ Forgetting to declare side income — all income must be declared
  • ❌ Not saving your receipt — always download and keep the acknowledgment

eCitizen Services Guide — Everything You Can Do Online

eCitizen is Kenya's one-stop government portal. Here's a complete guide to all the services you can access.

What is eCitizen?

eCitizen (ecitizen.go.ke) is Kenya's online platform for government services. Instead of visiting multiple government offices, you can access most services from one portal.

Services Available on eCitizen

  • Certificate of Good Conduct — required for jobs, visas, and licensing
  • Birth Certificate — application and replacement
  • Death Certificate — registration
  • Marriage Certificate — application
  • Passport Application — new and renewal
  • Driving License — new, renewal, and endorsement
  • Business Name Registration — sole proprietorship
  • Company Registration — limited companies

How to Register on eCitizen

  1. Visit ecitizen.go.ke
  2. Click "Create Account"
  3. Enter your ID number and KRA PIN
  4. Verify your email and phone number
  5. Set a password and log in

How to Write a CV That Gets You Hired in Kenya (2026)

Your CV is your first impression. Here's how to make it count — with tips from our AI-powered CV writing experience.

Why Most CVs Get Rejected

Recruiters spend an average of 6 seconds scanning a CV. If yours doesn't grab attention immediately, it goes to the "no" pile. Common mistakes include:

  • ❌ Too long (3+ pages for entry-level positions)
  • ❌ Generic — same CV for every job application
  • ❌ Poor formatting — hard to read, messy layout
  • ❌ Spelling and grammar errors
  • ❌ No quantified achievements — just listing duties

What Makes a Great CV

  • ✅ 1–2 pages maximum — concise and focused
  • ✅ Tailored to the job — match keywords from the job ad
  • ✅ Clean formatting — professional, easy to scan
  • ✅ Achievements, not duties — "Increased sales by 30%" not "Responsible for sales"
  • ✅ ATS-friendly — many companies use software to scan CVs
  • ✅ No errors — zero typos, perfect grammar

CV Structure for Kenyan Job Market

  1. Header: Name, phone, email, location
  2. Professional Summary: 2–3 sentences about you
  3. Work Experience: Most recent first, with achievements
  4. Education: Degrees, certifications, relevant courses
  5. Skills: Both technical and soft skills
  6. References: "Available on request" is fine

Business Registration in Kenya — Step by Step Guide (2026)

Starting a business in Kenya? Here's everything you need to know about registration, from sole proprietorship to limited companies.

Types of Business Registration in Kenya

  • Sole Proprietorship: Simplest form — one person owns the business. Registered through eCitizen.
  • Partnership: Two or more people. Registered through the Registrar of Business Names.
  • Limited Company: Separate legal entity. More complex but offers liability protection.

How to Register a Sole Proprietorship

  1. Choose a business name — make sure it's unique
  2. Search the name on eCitizen to confirm availability
  3. Register on eCitizen — fill in the BN1 form
  4. Pay the fee — KSh 1,000 via M-Pesa
  5. Download your certificate — usually processed in 1–3 days

After Registration — Don't Forget

  • 📌 Get a KRA PIN for your business
  • 📌 Register for relevant taxes (VAT, turnover tax)
  • 📌 Open a business bank account
  • 📌 Get any necessary licenses (county, sector-specific)
