# How to Register a Business in Kenya — Step by Step Guide (2026)

*Last updated: July 2026*

Starting a business in Kenya? Congratulations — *hongera!* Whether it's a shop in Nyatike town, a matatu business, a farm produce venture, or a tech startup, registering your business is the first real step toward legitimacy.

This guide walks you through every option, every step, and every cost — so you can register your business the right way, the first time.

## Why Register Your Business?

You might be thinking, *"My business is fine without registration. I've been running it for years."* Fair point. But here's why registration matters:

1. **Legal protection** — A registered business is a separate legal entity. Your personal assets are protected.
2. **Access to tenders** — Government and corporate tenders require registered businesses with valid documentation.
3. **Bank accounts** — Most banks won't open a business account without registration documents.
4. **M-Pesa Till/Paybill** — You need registration to get an M-Pesa business number.
5. **Loans and credit** — Banks and SACCOs require registration before lending.
6. **Customer trust** — A registered business looks more professional and trustworthy.
7. **Tax compliance** — You'll need a KRA PIN for your business, which requires registration.

*Kama unataka biashara ikue, ianze na msingi mzuri.* (If you want your business to grow, start with a solid foundation.)

## Types of Business Registration in Kenya

### 1. Sole Proprietorship (Business Name)
**Best for:** Small businesses, freelancers, single owners
- Simplest and cheapest to register
- You and the business are legally the same
- Good for: shops, salons, boda boda businesses, small farms
- **Cost:** Around KES 1,000–2,000 (government fees)

### 2. Partnership
**Best for:** Two or more people running a business together
- Partners share profits, losses, and liability
- Needs a partnership deed (we can help draft this)
- **Cost:** Around KES 2,000–3,000

### 3. Limited Company (Ltd)
**Best for:** Medium to large businesses, businesses seeking investment
- Separate legal entity from owners
- Limited liability — your personal assets are protected
- More paperwork but more credibility
- **Cost:** Around KES 10,000–25,000 (depending on share capital)

### 4. Company Limited by Guarantee
**Best for:** NGOs, non-profits, community organizations
- No share capital
- Members guarantee a nominal amount
- **Cost:** Varies

## Step-by-Step: Registering a Business Name (Sole Proprietorship)

This is the most common registration for small businesses in Nyatike and across Migori County.

### Step 1: Search for Your Business Name
- Go to **ecitizen.go.ke**
- Navigate to **Business Registration Service**
- Search for your desired business name
- Make sure it's not already taken
- **Cost:** KES 150 for the search

**Tips for choosing a business name:**
- Keep it simple and memorable
- Avoid names too similar to existing businesses
- Make sure it reflects what you do
- Consider how it sounds when said aloud

### Step 2: Reserve the Name
- If the name is available, reserve it
- **Cost:** KES 150
- The reservation lasts for 30 days

### Step 3: Fill the Registration Form
- Go back to eCitizen → Business Registration Service
- Select **"Business Name Registration"**
- Fill in:
  - Business name
  - Nature of business
  - Business address (your physical location in Nyatike or wherever)
  - Owner details (your national ID, KRA PIN, etc.)

### Step 4: Pay the Registration Fee
- **Cost:** KES 1,000 (registration) + KES 50 (filing fee)
- Pay via M-Pesa Paybill 206206

### Step 5: Download Your Certificate
- Once approved (usually 1-3 days), download your **Business Name Registration Certificate**
- This is your proof of registration

### Step 6: Get Your KRA PIN for the Business
- Register for a business KRA PIN on iTax
- This is separate from your personal PIN
- Needed for tax filing and compliance

## Step-by-Step: Registering a Limited Company

This is more involved but gives you more protection and credibility.

### Step 1: Name Search and Reservation
Same as above — search and reserve your company name on eCitizen.

### Step 2: Prepare the Required Documents
You'll need:
- **Memorandum of Understanding** — Why you're forming the company
- **Articles of Association** — How the company will be run
- **Form CR1** — Application for registration
- **Form CR2** — Notice of registered address
- **Form CR8** — Director and secretary details
- **Statement of nominal capital**

### Step 3: Submit and Pay
Upload all documents on eCitizen and pay the registration fee. Fees depend on your share capital:
- Up to KES 100,000 share capital: ~KES 10,000
- KES 100,001 – 500,000: ~KES 15,000
- Above KES 500,000: ~KES 20,000+

### Step 4: Receive Your Certificate
Once approved, you'll get:
- Certificate of Incorporation
- Company PIN
- CR12 (list of directors)

## After Registration — What's Next?

Once your business is registered, you still need:

1. **KRA Tax Compliance Certificate** — For tenders and credibility
2. **County Business Permit** — From Migori County Government (costs vary by business type and location)
3. **M-Pesa Till/Paybill** — Apply through Safaricom
4. **Business Bank Account** — With your registration documents
5. **NHIF for employees** — If you have staff
6. **NSSF for employees** — Mandatory for employers
