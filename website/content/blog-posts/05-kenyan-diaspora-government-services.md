# Kenyans in the Diaspora: How to Get Government Services Done Back Home

*Last updated: July 2026*

Over 3 million Kenyans live and work abroad — in the US, UK, Canada, Australia, the Middle East, and beyond. Whether you left for work, school, or a better life, one thing stays the same: Kenya still needs you to handle certain paperwork.

KRA returns. Birth certificates. Property documents. Business registrations. These things don't stop mattering just because you're in a different time zone.

This guide shows you exactly how to handle Kenyan government services from abroad — without booking a flight.

## The Diaspora Paperwork Problem

Let's be honest about the challenges:

- **Time zones** — Government offices work 8 AM – 5 PM East Africa Time. If you're in the US, that's the middle of the night.
- **Physical presence requirements** — Some services "require" you to be there, but there are workarounds.
- **Unreliable help** — Asking relatives to handle your paperwork is hit-or-miss. They're busy. They don't know the process. Things get delayed.
- **Changing systems** — Kenya's government services are constantly being digitized. What worked two years ago might not work now.
- **Trust issues** — Sharing your personal documents and KRA details with someone back home feels risky.

*Sasa, uko mbali — lakini kazi yako bado iko nyumbani.* (Now, you're far — but your work is still at home.)

- Your deadline (if any)

### Step 2: We Confirm and Quote
We'll tell you:
- Exactly what we need from you

- Expected turnaround time
- Payment instructions

### Step 3: You Pay via M-Pesa
If you have Safaricom M-Pesa:
- Send payment to our M-Pesa number
- Or use M-Pesa Global from abroad

If you don't have M-Pesa:
- Have someone in Kenya pay on your behalf
- We can accept bank transfer for larger amounts

### Step 4: We Do the Work
Our team in Nyatike processes your request. We:
- Visit government offices if needed
- Submit applications online
- Follow up on status
- Send you updates via WhatsApp at every step

### Step 5: We Deliver
- **Digital documents** (PDFs, screenshots): Sent via WhatsApp or Email
- **Physical documents** (certificates, licences): We can:
  - Hold for pickup by your designated person
  - Send via courier (cost varies)
  - Scan and send digital copies first

## Common Questions from Diaspora Clients

**Q: How do I send you my documents from abroad?**
A: WhatsApp is easiest. Just take a clear photo of your ID, birth certificate, or any document and send it to us. We can also receive via email.

**Q: Is it safe to share my KRA PIN and password?**
A: We understand the concern. Many of our diaspora clients share these details with us for annual KRA filing. We use them only for the specific service you've requested and never store passwords after the job is done.

**Q: What if I need something done urgently?**
A: Tell us your deadline. We handle urgent requests regularly. Some services (like KRA returns or nil returns) can be done same-day. Others depend on government processing times.

**Q: How do I pay if I don't have M-Pesa?**
A: Options include: M-Pesa Global (works in many countries), having a relative in Kenya pay on your behalf, or bank transfer for larger amounts.

**Q: What if the service takes longer than expected?**
A: Government timelines are sometimes unpredictable. We'll keep you updated on progress and follow up with the relevant offices. We never leave you wondering.

**Q: Can I get a refund if the service fails?**
A: If we fail to deliver due to our error, we offer a full refund. If the government rejects an application due to your documents or eligibility, our service fee stands but we'll help you reapply.
