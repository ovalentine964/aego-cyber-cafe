# How to File KRA Returns in 2026 — Complete Guide for Migori County

*Last updated: July 2026*

Every year, millions of Kenyans are required to file their KRA returns by June 30th. If you're in Nyatike, Migori County, and wondering how to go about it — this guide is for you. We'll walk you through the process step by step, whether you're employed, self-employed, or running a business.

## What Are KRA Returns and Why Do They Matter?

KRA returns are documents you submit to the Kenya Revenue Authority showing your income and tax payments for the previous year. If you have a KRA PIN, you're legally required to file returns — even if you earned zero income (that's called a nil return).

**Why it matters:**
- Late filing attracts a penalty of KES 2,000 or 25% of the tax due (whichever is higher)
- You need a Tax Compliance Certificate (TCC) for government tenders, job applications, and bank loans
- Non-compliance can result in your PIN being deactivated

As they say in Kiswahili, *bila kodi, hakuna maisha mazuri* — without taxes, there's no good life. Filing keeps you on the right side of the law.

## Types of KRA Returns

### 1. Employment Income Returns (IT1)
This is for salaried employees. Your employer deducts PAYE (Pay As You Earn) monthly, and you file an annual return confirming everything.

### 2. Business Income Returns (IT2)
For business owners, traders, and anyone self-employed. You declare your business income and expenses.

### 3. Rental Income Returns
If you earn rental income from property, you file this return. Even if you rent out a single room in Nyatike town, you're supposed to declare it.

### 4. Nil Returns
If you have a KRA PIN but earned no income during the year, you still file a nil return. It takes 2 minutes and saves you a KES 2,000 penalty.

## How to File KRA Returns Online — Step by Step

### Step 1: Log into iTax
Go to **itax.kra.go.ke** and log in with your KRA PIN and password. If you've forgotten your password, you can reset it — or visit a cyber cafe like Aego to get help.

### Step 2: Download the Return Form
Once logged in, go to **Returns** → **File Return** → Select the type of return (employment, business, nil). Download the Excel template.

### Step 3: Fill in the Template
The Excel form has several sheets:
- **Sheet 1:** Your basic details (pre-filled)
- **Sheet 2:** Income details — enter your gross salary or business income
- **Sheet 3:** Deductions — insurance, mortgage interest, pension contributions
- **Sheet 4:** Tax credits — PAYE already deducted by your employer

### Step 4: Upload and Submit
Save the completed Excel file, go back to iTax, upload it, and click submit. You'll get a confirmation receipt — download it and keep it safe.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not filing at all** — The biggest mistake. Even a nil return is better than nothing.
2. **Wrong income figures** — Use your P9 form (for employees) or proper records (for businesses).
3. **Missing the deadline** — June 30th every year. Mark it on your phone now.
4. **Forgetting to declare all income** — Side hustles, rental income, freelance work — it all counts.
5. **Not keeping records** — Save your receipts, P9 forms, and confirmation receipts.
